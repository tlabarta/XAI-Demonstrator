Layer-wise Relevance Propagation (LRP) is a technique that brings such explainability and scales to potentially highly complex deep neural networks. It operates by propagating the prediction backward in the neural network, using a set of purposely designed propagation rules (Montavon et al. 2019).

Concept Relevance Propagation (CRP) is a cutting-edge explainability method for deep neural networks that allows for the understanding of AI predictions on the concept level. As an advancement of LRP, CRP indicates not only which input features are relevant, but also communicates which concepts are used, where they are located in the input and which parts of the neural network are responsible (Samek et al.).
